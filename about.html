About me

Since I was a little girl, I have loved to create. From designing my own clothes, making home decor and furniture, to being a mini MacGiver- looking for solutions for all sorts of issues I might meet along the way.

As I grew older I decided to go a more academic route, focusing on my interest in Web design. Followed by my interest in how to help companies create websites, where they establish a digital presecence - I studied Information Technology and started my career as a Web developer.

As I have gotten older, I have realized the importance of doing what you love, not just as a hobby, but through what you spend the most time on - your job.

Working and caring for people, solving problems, and being creative is what I love, and that's why I decided to transition into UX and UI design - and it's one of the best decisions I have made.

To me and as a UX/UI designer, the combination of psychology, business, and design is ideal in the process of solving problems and meeting both the user and the business needs.

If I'm not doing UX/UI, I'm either creating digital art and illustrations or just hanging out with my friends and family.
