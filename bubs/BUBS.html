BUBS

BUBS is designed for parents to have a fun and healthy
social life during parental leave, by providing a platform
for parents to find and connect with others in the same situation.

Problem

Imagine, you have just had a baby, maybe for the first time. Your partner is back at work, and you are all alone with your baby while everyone you know is at work. 

Being at home alone with a baby during parental leave is proven to increase the risk of feeling situational loneliness. Research shows that many parents report meeting friends less often after having a baby.

Knowing where and how to get in contact with others in the same situation can be challenging, and I have found that many feel uncomfortable approaching other parents, in parks, on playgrounds, etc., and initiate to hang out. 

Solution

BUBS is an app designed to help parents find and connect with other parents on parental leave, by providing a platform where you can see others nearby, find parents with similar interests, send messages, and plan to meet up in an easy, fun, and effortless way.

My Role

This is the result of an academic project as a part of Google UX certificate. I have worked as a solo designer, in good collaboration with my mentor. I have been responsible for the whole end-to-end process and was given the freedom to come up with the concept.

This has included  User Research, Ideation, User Stories, User Journeys, Information Architecture, Sketching, Wireframes, Wireflows, Visual Design, Prototyping, and Usability testing.

01 Research

Interviews

5 interviews, as well as a diary study, were conducted with parents who are or have been on parental leave for the last 3 years, and here are my main findings.

Loneliness

I found that many parents experience loneliness while being on parental leave. Many reported not having friends in the same situation, and therefore not having many to hang out with during parental leave. 

Arenas to meet

There is a lack of arenas for parents to meet and connect with others on parental leave. Although there are organized “maternity groups”, people often end up not going for various reasons. There are very few organized options for dads. 

Uncertain in the role

Many expressed that they felt uncertain about what they were doing and that they wanted a place to ask questions and share experiences with people who could relate. 

Social Barrier

Although parents would meet others on playgrounds or other activities, many reported not feeling comfortable being the one to initiate to hang out. 

01 Synthesise & Analyze

I then started analyzing the data gathered from the research phase. I was then able to see patterns and gain valuable insight that helped me really define the users and the problem they are facing. I started by creating an affinity map and empathy maps.

Affinity Map

I created an Affinity Map to start organizing and making sense of the data collected in the research phase. This helped me discover patterns and getting a better understanding of the users and the foundation of the problem they were experiencing.

Empathy Maps

Another method I used to organize insights and observations and to really try to understand the user's pain points, goals, feelings, thoughts, and behaviors, was empathy maps.

Personas

I created three personas to represent the user, which I used as a guide to making design decisions and priorities. Keeping these personas in mind throughout the design process, helped me keep the users in the center of the design.

01 Usability Testing

Two rounds of usability testing were conducted with five participants in each round. This led to further iterations and improvements. 

Main Issues

  • Profile cards being confusing and didn't provide enough info for the user to make a decision 

  • Some participants expressed that the instructions were unclear

  • 80% did not see an important button

  • More feedback required

  • Visibility and comprehension of icons

Iterations

  • Create a bigger interaction area so it is easier for the user to swipe card, make them bigger and look more like cards, and possible to see some of the next cards

  • Provide more information to the closed cards to give more information to base their decision on

  • Word the instructions in a simple way to reduce cognitive load, and create more contrast to the background.

  • Make the skip for now button more visible, and move closer to the next button 

  • Give feedback when swiping the open card

  • Change icons

01 Main Features

Profile Cards

Onboarding process to set up your personal profile and preferences. People can then see you and send a connections request based on your common interests and life situation.

02 Learnings

Done is better than perfect

It is easy to get caught up in the details and spend a lot of time on making things perfect, which you most likely have to change at a later stage. Rather get on with the process, and make things pixel perfect after feedback and iterations are done. 

Avoid getting too attached

Being able to stay true to the Human-Centered Design Process, means always being open to changing your ideas and design no matter how much you love it. Keep the user in the center, always.  

Stay Organized

Staying organized from the beginning will save you a lot of time. 

Test, test & test again

I have learned a lot from all my test sessions and been able to see things through the eyes of the users. Things that might be obvious to me, might not be obvious to the user. Getting continuous feedback is invaluable.

Design Handoff

The next step would be to hand over the designs to the developers. To minimize confusion and back-and-forth communication, as well as assure the quality of the product - good communication with the developers throughout the project is vital. Some important aspects would be:

  • To ensure a smooth handover, use tools such as Zeplin, Invision, or Marvel. 

  • Include User Flows to help the developers have a clear understanding of how the flow of the implementation should look like

  • Add a video showing micro-interactions between or within screens

  • Make sure to include updated versions of the Style Guide

  • Have good communication with everyone involved and asked for a review of the design deliverables, to avoid confusion at a later stage
