FitMates

A fitness app that lets you reach your health and fitness goals together with friends and family in a fun, social and encouraging way.

Challenge

Imagine, you are using a fitness app and have just achieved your weekly fitness goal or found a fun fitness activity happening next Tuesday in your area, and you really want to share it with your friends and family. 

FitMates is a health and fitness tracking app, for family and friends. Currently, there is no way for users to communicate or alert others about achievements and goals.

This has led to a high percentage of user drop-off because the users aren’t experiencing the app's social elements to be useful.

The hypothesis is that by integrating a messaging feature where users can message each other at any point of the app experience, FitMates will reach its overall goal of increased engagement and repeat usage.

Project

This is the result of an academic project as a part of Google UX Certificate. I was given a scenario and asked to solve the problem for a fictional company.

Duration: 6 weeks

My Role

Being the solo designer, I was responsible for the end-to-end process, this has included  User Research, Ideation, User Journeys, Information Architecture, Sketching, Wireframes, Visual Design, Prototyping, Usability testing. 

Solution

The outcome of the process is an integrated messaging feature that allows users to motivate each other and exercise together, as well as sharing and celebrating each other's achievements and goals, without compromising on the concern of privacy exposure. 

FitMates is designed to help people reach their fitness goals in a fun, social, and encouraging way.

01 Research

Although I would usually have primary research and user interviews as a part of my process, I decided to do a competitive analysis, secondary research, and market research for this project.

This was due to time constraint, but also due to the fact that there has already been done large numbers of studies on what leads to sustained and repeat engagement of Fitness Apps.

Competitive Analysis

I started off my research by doing a quick competitive analysis. This was done to identify successful features that could be used as inspiration for the next step of the process, as well as redundant features to avoid, that made the apps feel cluttered.

Secondary Research

Secondary Research based on academic research was conducted, to get a better understanding of the problem and the users. I started the research by asking myself a few questions, and by generating a hypothesis.

“I’d like to share my exercise status.… If someone gives me a thumbs-up, I will be very happy and it will improve my exercise efficiency.”  

(Interviewee, Zhanga & Xu, 2020)

Market Research

I did market research to get a better understanding of the health and fitness app market. This also allowed me to gain valuable information about what drives the market and helped me gain insight into elements that could be beneficial to include to meet the market needs.

02 Information Architecture

User Flows

I then started to create User Flows. This was done to identify what routes were going to be critical to the design, and therefore should be in an MVP. It also helped me to get a visual representation of what routes the users would take to reach their goals.

Red Route 1-4 was created at an early stage, and Route 5 was incorporated at a later stage as a result of feedback from the guerilla testing. 

03 Sketching

I decided to sketch out the red routes, before making it digital. This helped me to visualize and work through my ideas and concepts, but it also allowed me to do quick iterations where needed.  

04 Wireframes

I was then ready to start creating low-fidelity wireframes. It was important for me to find a balance between not spending too much time on the details, but at the same time incorporate enough to create a realistic experience as the next step of the process was Guerilla Testing.

05 Guerilla Test

I continued by creating a prototype in Invision to test my design. Doing a Guerilla Test with 4 participants, allowed me to get some valuable feedback from the users at an early stage, and I quickly realized what iterations I needed to make. Below are the main issues and iterations.

Issues

  • No way to schedule video workouts

  • Uncertainty about whether pictures are shared or not

  • Text is too small

  • The profile is viewed from other users perspective

  • Users had a hard time finding the messages

Iterations

  • Create calendar invite function

  • Animated feedback - picture “goes into” story

  • Change font size

  • Change profile view to profile owners view

  • Change icon, and move achievements section to the home screen

01 Usability Testing

Another round of usability testing was conducted with five participants. This time the participants were people who fit the target group, and who are using or have been using fitness apps. New issues were identified, and iterations were done.

Issues

  • No way to create a new message

  • The post-workout photo flow seems incomplete, and users should be automatically redirected to home-screen

  • Privacy Concerns - there should be a way to control who can see your information and photos - this is where user flows 5 was created 

  • “Invite” and “Video workout" should be merged

  • Too many elements on the post-workout photo screen

  • Photo instructions are unclear and users aren't reading it

  • Some text and icons appear too small

Iterations

  • Add a create new message button

  • Automatically redirect the user to Home Screen after taking a post-workout photo

  • Create a Privacy Setting flow, where users can choose who to share their data in a simple way.

  • Merge “Invite” and “Video workout”.

  • Make two separate screens for completion feedback and camera

  • Change instructions to only be shown to first-time users, and add an exit button so it won’t disappear before the user has understood the instruction. 

  • Make text and icons bigger

Final Result

Messages

A messaging feature that allows users to communicate at any point of the experience.

Share A Post-Workout Photo

To make the experience more fun and engaging, I created a way for users to share a post-workout photo or icon with friends and family. 

Pop-Up Notifications

Pop-up notifications to notify about friends' and families' achievements, messages, personal progress, new workouts, etc.

Users will also receive likes and feedback, which will help them stay motivated.

Privacy Settings/Control

Through my research, I found users were concerned about their privacy.

I, therefore, created a way for users to have full control over who can see your information, achievements, and pictures by changing your privacy settings in a quick and simple way.

Video Workout Invite

To make the experience more engaging and social I created a way for users to invite others to do a video workout together.

This was also based on my findings in my market research, where I found that there has been an increase of 46% downloads of fitness apps globally, due to the pandemic.

This feature will allow users to work out together despite social distancing.

Final Thoughts

Learnings

Ensure data-driven design decisions

In the initial stage of the project, I quite quickly made assumptions that gamification would be the way to go, to create increased engagement in fitness apps. My assumptions were quickly proven to be wrong once I started the user research. This shows the importance of ensuring data-driven design decisions, to meet the actual needs of the users. 

Privacy of fitness-related data

Another interesting thing I learned was that people seemed especially concerned about having full control of who they shared their fitness-related data with. A recurring statement was that they only wanted to share this information with their closest friends and family, and not like on other social media platforms where users often have many peripheral friends.

Future Steps

The next step for this project would be to do more usability testing to ensure that the iterations that were done have solved the previously identified issues and to identify new potential issues. 

The goal of creating a new messaging feature was to create sustained engagement and repeat usage. Therefore it would also be important to set some metrics and measure the results to be able to see if the new features are successful. 

Additional Features

It could also be interesting to explore some additional features. 

Mascot

To further develop the Brand Personality, it could be fun to incorporate a “Mascot” into the brand. This could be used in Pop-Up Notifications and as a little helper that reminds you and encourages you to work out and to continue to work towards your weekly goals. 

Challenges

Although I found that competition could have negative consequences in fitness apps, if used in the right way, I think it could help motivate people to keep exercising and continue to use the app. An idea could be to let users invite each other to do challenges. However, this would need to be done in a way where you avoid having people comparing themselves to each other and rather focus on personal goals and achievements. 

Design Handoff

The next step would be to hand over the designs to the developers. To minimize confusion and back-and-forth communication, as well as assure the quality of the product - good communication with the developers throughout the project is vital. Some important aspects would be: 

  • To ensure a smooth handover, use tools such as Zeplin, Invision, or Zeplin 

  • Include user flows to help the developers have a clear understanding of how the flow of the implementation should look like

  • Add a video showing micro-interactions between or within screens

  • Make sure to include updated versions of the Style Guide

  • Have good communication with everyone involved and asked for a review of the design deliverables, to avoid confusion at a later stage
